Internet Traffic

The internet traffic is estimated to grow between 2015-2020 by 20.1%; this represents the volume of the total data sent across the internet globally per month. Based on data provided by Cisco, the average internet user generate 15.5 gigabytes per month in 2014 an increase from 12.4 gigabytes per month in 2013 (25.0% increase). Even with the net neutrality laws implemented in some areas of the world, there is still an rapid upward trend of the internet traffic volumes due to the rapid growth in mobile video traffic volumes.

Outlook

The internet traffic volume is expected to continue to grow to 2020. More and more people around the world will be able to easily access the internet. In addition, internet access will be more convenient in the developed world.

Internet Data Capacity

The expansion of Internet data capacity allowed services like video streaming, cloud data storage and online video games to easily be enjoyed by the average user. Aiding this effect is the low cost of internet services, usually the only fixed cost in internet-based services. The recession brought on the largest adoption of Internet services as businesses cut costs and consumers looked for the best price for services they demanded.

Outlook

Over the next five years until 2020, the percentage of services conducted online will increase. The rising data capacity of the internet will open up new online services, such as cloud data storage and video streaming services. Digital advertising budgets will surpass TV and spending in 2016, inciting more companies to conduct business online.
